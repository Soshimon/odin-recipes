Milk Tea

Photo by Howief licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Unported license.

Sweet, creamy, and refreshing.

Milk tea is a popular drink made by combining strong black tea with milk and sugar. It’s smooth, lightly caffeinated, and can be enjoyed hot or cold. Many variations exist around the world, from classic Hong Kong milk tea to bubble tea with chewy tapioca pearls.

Ingredients

  • 2 black tea bags (or 2 teaspoons loose-leaf tea)
  • 1 cup hot water
  • Â½ cup milk (whole or evaporated for richness)
  • 2-3 teaspoons sugar (adjust to taste)
  • Ice (optional)
  • Tapioca pearls (optional, for bubble tea style)

Steps

  1. Steep tea bags in hot water for 3-5 minutes until strong. Remove tea bags.
  2. Stir in sugar while tea is hot.
  3. Add milk and mix well.
  4. For iced milk tea, let cool and pour over ice.
  5. (Optional) Add cooked tapioca pearls before serving.
